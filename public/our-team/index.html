Yash Bajaj

Policy Practitioner and Advisor

Yash is advising the City of Boston in the outreach and advocacy of the Anti-Displacement Project. He is currently pursuing his Master in Public Policy (2024-2026) at Harvard Kennedy School, with a concentration in Political and Economic Development as well as the Data and Research track.

Yash Bajaj is a current Master in Public Policy student at the Harvard Kennedy School. He has over eight years of experience in India’s governance landscape, focusing on transforming large-scale public programs in elementary education, skill development, and workforce improvement. His work emphasizes building institutional capacities across state governments to enhance program delivery and achieve systemic impact. He has contributed to evidence-based policy design and implementation through roles with the National Skill Development Corporation, J-PAL South Asia, and Central Square Foundation. In addition to his work with the Executive branch, Yash has engaged with the Legislative branch as a legislative aide to a Member of Parliament, where he drafted constitutional amendments, analyzed legislation, and supported parliamentary procedures. A strong advocate of data-centric governance, Yash believes in leveraging technology and data-driven systems to improve state capacity and deliver high-quality, citizen-centric services. His recent projects include creating dashboards to track literacy program outcomes, designing competency-based digital and leadership training for government staff, and building India’s national labor market information system.
